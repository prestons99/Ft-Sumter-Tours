Barnacle

Habitat:

Barnacles are commonly found in the intertidal zone, rocky shores and estuaries. They are found attached to rocks, walls, stems of marsh grass, and boats. They have to live in areas where they are covered by the tide at least two times daily. They prefer to settle in areas already colonized by other barnacles.

Facts:

Class: Maxillopoda

Order: Sessilia - sessile barnacles

Family: Chtmanalidae

Description:

Barnacles are gray in coloration. Six overlapping plates of calcium carbonate surround their body. The body is segmented, head at the base, legs extended out in fan-like pattern. Cirri are modified legs that look like fans waving in the water, collect food and water. Adult form has no eyes or sensory appendages.

Size:

Adults can reach 1/8 inches (3 mm) for one diameter, 1/4 inches (5 mm) in another diameter.

Predators:

Snails, starfish, whelks and fish are commmon predators of the barnacle.

Diet/Prey:

Barnacles will feed on plankton and organic debris. They are filter feeders, and kick their legs out of their shells to collect food

Reproduction:

Barnacles are hermaphrodites (containing both sexes) but will cross fertilize (fertilize with another individual). In extreme cases will fertilize themselves. They will extended a tube out of their shell and into the shell of another; the eggs are fertilized and hatched inside that parent shell. Larvae live as zooplankton for weeks and will go through several molts before settling. They will change to the adult form after they attach themselves to a substrate.
